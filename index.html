The Solar System!

The Solar System is located within the Milky Way. It is made up of the Sun and all of the objects that orbit it, including the eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets, comets, and asteroids. The Sun, being at the center, exerts its gravitational force to keep everything in orbit.

The Sun

  • The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System.
  • Though the Sun appears yellow, orange, or red, it is actually white. Earth's atmosphere scatters its short-wavelength colors.
  • Composed of about 70% hydrogen and 28% helium. The other 1.5% is composed of carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen. The 0.5% consists of many elements, such as neon, iron, silicon, sulfur, and magnesium. These all create the hot plasma, which generates energy for life on Earth.
  • The Sun's gravity holds the entire Solar System together.
  • Bigger than 85% of all stars in the galaxy.
  • Light from the Sun takes about 20,000 years to travel from its core to the surface.

The Sun in Motion!

Mercury

  • Mercury has the largest core relative to its body of any planet
  • Mercury has the greatest temperature fluctuations of any planet
  • Mercury has the shortest orbital period of any planet
  • Mercury has only been visited by 3 spacecrafts
  • Mercury has more mass than Ganymede and Titan combined

Venus

  • Venus is the sister planet of Earth
  • It is the hottest planet out of the eight listed
  • The atmospheric pressure on Venus is 90 times that of Earth
  • Venus is 81% the mass of Earth
  • It is possible Venus might have once had water flowing on its surface

Earth

  • Where all life is
  • The largest of the rocky planets
  • More massive than all of the other rocky planets combined
  • The only planet with liquids on its surface
  • The planet with he biggest moon relative to its size

Mars

  • Known as the red planet
  • About half the size of Earth
  • Has the tallest volcano in the solar system: Olympus Mons
  • 1 of the only 2 inner planets to have at least one moon
  • Has the gravity value as Mercury despite being bigger

Jupiter

  • The biggest planet in the solar system
  • 3 times more massive than Saturn despite being a similar same radius
  • Most famous storm: the Great Red Spot
  • Has the most dynamic atmosphere of the gaseous planets
  • Has 4 of the 7 moons bigger than Pluto

Saturn

  • Known for its famous rings
  • The most moons of any planet at 146 currently
  • The least dense of any planet
  • Source of a storm bigger than the GRS called the Great White Spot
  • Saturn's ring tilt from Earth varies throughout years

Uranus

  • Coldest recorded temperature of any planet
  • Has moons that are named after Shakespearean characters
  • Has a lower surface gravity than Earth does
  • Most unique rotation of any planet
  • Its moons and rings are also tilted similarly

Neptune

  • Fastest winds of any planet
  • Around the same temperature as Uranus despite being much further
  • Has a very major moon that orbits in the opposite direction Neptune spins
  • Not as blue as commonly depicted
  • Like Uranus, Neptune was only visited by 1 spacecraft
